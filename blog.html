FOOD WASTE MANAGEMENT

Food waste management is the process of reducing, recovering, and recycling food that is not consumed by humans or animals. It aims to prevent food loss and waste, which can have negative impacts on the environment, economy, and society.

The paper aims at explaining the issue of food waste and describing approaches to the reduction of this problem. The introduction contains a general overview of the paper and presents the components included in the study. Research contains an expanded definition of the ‘food waste’ concept along with its presence in foodservice organizations. In the conclusion, the main points of the paper are summarized. The research presents a brief but substantial analysis of the food waste problem and approaches to its elimination.

FOOD WASTE REDUCTION

Reducing food waste is crucial for environmental sustainability and ensuring efficient resource use. Here are several methods to help reduce food waste at different stages of the food supply chain and in households: Meal Planning: Plan your meals in advance. Create a shopping list based on planned meals to avoid impulse purchases. Storage Practices: Properly store perishable items to extend their shelf life. Use airtight containers to keep food fresh. Be aware of the shelf life of different products and consume them before they expire. First In, First Out (FIFO): Practice FIFO in both your pantry and refrigerator. Use older items before newer ones. Portion Control: Serve and consume reasonable portions to avoid leftovers. Teach and encourage portion control, especially in restaurants.

Understand Expiration Dates: Understand the difference between "sell by," "use by," and "best by" dates. Many products are still safe to consume after the expiration date. Donate Surplus Food: Contribute excess food to local food banks or charities. Consider joining food-sharing platforms to connect with individuals or organizations in need. Composting: Compost kitchen scraps and food waste to reduce landfill impact. Set up a composting system at home or support community composting initiatives. Educational Campaigns: Raise awareness about food waste through educational programs. Encourage responsible consumption and disposal practices. Technology Solutions: Use mobile apps to track food inventory and expiration dates. Employ smart storage solutions that help monitor and manage food freshness. Recovery of Imperfect Produce: Support initiatives that utilize "ugly" or imperfect produce to reduce waste. Collaboration Along the Supply Chain: Encourage collaboration between producers, retailers, and consumers to streamline the supply chain and reduce waste at each stage.
